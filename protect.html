Protect Yourself!

Too Good to Be True

Many Phishers present deals that seem to be unreasonably good to draw your attention to them. An example of this may be them saying that you won a lottery and just need to pay a fee for it to be sent to your account. Remember if a deal seems too good to be true.

Sense of Urgency

A common tactic for Phishers is a sense of urgency that encourages you to listen to them now. This makes it easier for you to miss small hints that may not be a legitimate encounter. Some may say that you need to input your credit card details in the next 5 minutes or you won't be able to claim the $30000 you just won.

Hyperlinks

The text that a link displays is not always the url that it brings you to. Hover over a link to check where it will bring you. Phishers often use tactics to disguise the like like using a r and n to make something that looks like a m but brings you to another location like BankofArnerica.com

Attachments

If an email comes with an unexpected attachment dont open it. Most file types can be dangerous one way or another. The only fully safe file type is .txt.

Unusual Sender

If you aren't expecting an email it's likely a scam of some sort. So be cautious when opening any unexpected emails.
